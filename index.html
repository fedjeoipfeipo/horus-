5,000 Years of Symbolism

The Sacred Scarab A Living Symbol

In ancient Egypt, the scarab was not mere ornament. It was the embodiment of Khepri the god of the rising sun, of transformation, of forces too vast to name. Pharaohs wore it as armor. Priests carved it into tomb walls. Warriors pressed it into wax seals before battle.

Azza Fahmy does not recreate history. She continues it. Each scarab ring is hand-engraved in Cairo, set with ethically sourced stones, and carries the same weight of meaning it has always carried — across five thousand years of human civilization.
