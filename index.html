The Scarab

In ancient Egypt, the scarab beetle was Khepri — god of the rising sun. It represented transformation, protection, and the eternal cycle of rebirth. Pharaohs wore it to announce their divinity.

𓋹

The Symbol of Eternity

For over five thousand years, the scarab seal was pressed into gold to mark ownership, power, and sacred intention. These seals were buried with kings to protect their passage into the afterlife.

𓇳

Carried Forward

Azza Fahmy began researching the Egyptian collection in 1988. After twelve years of study — eight in research, three in design — she translated this sacred symbol into fine jewelry for the modern world.
